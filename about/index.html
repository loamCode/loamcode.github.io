About Me

Born and raised in Kansas, USA, I find a unique joy in seeking to understand of our modern food system. Food is intrinsic to civilization, culture, and humanity. To solve problems in this field is to investigate how the world works, humans and nature together. I fell in love with natural systems while at Kansas State University, where I obtained a Bachelor of Science in Microbiology.

Endeavoring to become an infectious disease epidemiologist, I then undertook a Master in Public Health at Emory University's Rollins School of Public Health. While attending, I functioned as a food safety analyst at my practicum home site, the Georgia Department of Agriculture. There I became fascinated with traceback investigations. I felt that if we could only digitize these systems, network-based technologies could ameliorate entrenched challenges in distribution, safety, and public health.

In 2018, I started consulting for the Institute of Food Technologists' Global Food Traceability Center, a position where I would grow to become a known authority on standardized food traceability. I honed these skills at Chipotle Mexican Grill, where I led the technical implementation of RFID-based tracking, among other projects. In 2023, the US Food and Drug Administration tapped me to serve as their lead technical expert on digital food traceability within the Office of the Commissoner.

Later in 2023, I decided that I wanted to conduct my own research activities.
